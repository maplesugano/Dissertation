The Key Finding: V >> A/D
The Pearson gap is the most important observation. The model has learned valence (positive/negative sentiment is strongly lexically encoded), but arousal and dominance are barely above chance. On the sample outputs you can see it clearly — predictions like V=3.44 A=3.33 D=3.44 for multiple different sentences suggest the model is regressing toward a near-mean cluster for A and D rather than truly predicting them.

This is actually the perfect motivation for SIM-CoT: the 3 reasoning steps are explicitly structured as V-step → A-step → D-step. SIM-CoT's auxiliary decoder supervises each latent with its corresponding dimension's step, which should force the latent tokens to encode A-specific and D-specific semantics rather than collapsing into a shared representation dominated by valence.

Expected SIM-CoT improvement target: bring A and D Pearson from 0.34 up toward the V level (0.68), which would align with the paper's reported +4–8% gains on GSM8k.
